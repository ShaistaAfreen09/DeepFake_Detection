Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is the purpose of this application? +

This application is designed to help detect and classify deepfake content in images, videos, and audio files. It uses advanced machine learning models to identify manipulated or artificially generated media, helping users verify the authenticity of digital content.

How does the app measure the "fakeness" of content? +

The app analyzes the content and provides a "fakeness" score in terms of percentage. After analysis, it generates a detailed report showing how likely the content is to be fake based on the model's classification results.

What is the accuracy of the deepfake detection model? +

Our model has been trained with a wide range of deepfake data and achieves an accuracy of around 95%, ensuring a high level of reliability when detecting fake media.

Who developed this deepfake classification model? +

The model was created by a dedicated team of AI researchers, software engineers, data scientists, and designers. You can learn more about the developer team in the About Us section.

What types of content can this app analyze? +

The app is capable of detecting deepfakes in images, videos, and audio files. It uses separate models trained specifically to identify manipulations in each type of media.

Is the app suitable for personal and professional use? +

Yes, this app is suitable for both personal use (e.g., verifying content shared on social media) and professional use (e.g., verifying the authenticity of digital media for journalism or legal purposes).

How can I trust the results provided by the model? +

While no model is 100% perfect, our model's high accuracy and comprehensive reporting system ensure that the detection results are trustworthy. We encourage users to always review the detailed reports provided.

What will happen if the video containing no faces is uploaded? +

If a video with no faces is uploaded, the deepfake detection model may not analyze it effectively, as it is primarily trained to detect manipulated facial features. In such cases, the app may indicate a low likelihood of deepfake manipulation or notify that no faces were found.
